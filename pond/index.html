Pond

A ripple sequencer for iPad, built on one idea: drop a stone in the water and let the wave play the notes. Every cell holds a note; when a ripple's wave front crosses it, it sounds. Where you tap and how the waves overlap — that's the composition.

What it does

The idea

Waves play the notes

Tap anywhere and a wave expands from your finger, sounding every note it crosses. Use several fingers for several waves. The interference between them is the rhythm.

Emitters

Plant springs that ripple in time

Hold a finger still for half a second to plant an emitter — a spring that keeps dropping ripples on its own, locked to the tempo. Up to eight, for evolving polyrhythms.

Feel

Liquid, or locked tight

With quantize off, notes sound the instant the wave touches them — pure water physics. Switch it to ⅛ or 1/16 and the waves stay liquid while the rhythm snaps to the grid.

Notes

Any scale, any key

Nine scales and any key, within an octave range you set. Lay a scale across the whole pond, or Randomize a fresh one at whatever density you like.

Connected

Standalone & AUv3

Play on its own with a built-in CoreMIDI source, or load it as an AUv3 MIDI processor inside AUM, Logic, and Cubasis. Sends and follows MIDI clock, and follows host tempo.

Sound

A voice of its own

A soft 16-voice pluck is built in, so Pond makes sound out of the box — or switch it off and let it drive your favourite synths instead.

A few more touches

Reflections, presets, and painting

Turn on wall reflections and waves bounce off the pond's edges, re-playing cells on the way back — one tap becomes a conversation between a wave and its echoes. Save ponds to preset slots and jump between them live (waves in flight roll right over the new notes). And paint notes straight onto the water from a floating palette, coloured by pitch — warm peach for C through to sky blue for B.
